# Couch-to-10-Miles Cycling Plan

**Starting point:** ~11.3 mph for 1 mile, not gassed
**Goal:** 10 miles at 15 mph pace (40 minutes) by end of summer
**Schedule:** 3 rides per week, ~14 weeks
**Top priority:** healthy knees

---

## The two rules that protect your knees

1. **Spin, don't grind.** Stay in an easier gear and pedal faster (aim ~80–90 rpm). Forcing speed in a hard gear is the #1 cause of beginner knee pain — and spinning is also how you get faster.
2. **Pain rules:** sharp knee pain = stop for the day. Dull muscle fatigue = normal, keep going. Warm up at an easy spin for the first 3–5 minutes of every ride.

The recovery weeks (4, 8) are deliberate. Don't skip them — that's where your joints and tendons actually adapt.

---

## How to read the paces

- **Easy** — conversational, you could talk in full sentences (~10–12 mph). High cadence.
- **Steady** — working but controlled, breathing harder (~13–14 mph).
- **Brisk** — pushing, short bursts at 15+ mph.

Each week has three rides: a short easy spin, your longer endurance ride, and a "quality" ride that becomes your speed work later on.

---

## Phase 1 — Build the base (Weeks 1–4)
*Goal: get comfortable, lock in high cadence, zero knee pain. Pace doesn't matter at all yet.*

| Week | Ride A (easy) | Ride B (endurance) | Ride C (quality) | Long ride |
|------|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------|
| 1 | 2 mi easy | 3 mi easy | 2.5 mi easy | 3 mi |
| 2 | 2.5 mi easy | 4 mi easy | 3 mi easy | 4 mi |
| 3 | 3 mi easy | 5 mi easy | 3 mi easy + 3 × 20-sec light pickups* | 5 mi |
| 4 (recovery) | 2 mi very easy | 4 mi easy | 3 mi easy | 4 mi |

\*Pickups = spin your legs faster for 20 seconds in an easy gear, not a harder gear. Pure leg speed.

---

## Phase 2 — Build the distance (Weeks 5–8)
*Goal: stretch the long ride toward 8–9 miles, all easy. Start sprinkling in steady efforts.*

| Week | Ride A (easy) | Ride B (endurance) | Ride C (quality) | Long ride |
|------|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------|
| 5 | 3 mi easy | 6 mi easy | 3.5 mi easy + 4 × 20-sec pickups | 6 mi |
| 6 | 3 mi easy | 7 mi easy | 4 mi, incl. 2 × 3-min steady | 7 mi |
| 7 | 3 mi easy | 8 mi easy | 4 mi, incl. 3 × 3-min steady | 8 mi |
| 8 (recovery) | 3 mi easy | 5 mi easy | 4 mi easy | 5 mi |

---

## Phase 3 — Reach 10 miles (Weeks 9–10)
*Goal: complete 10 miles. Pace still doesn't matter — just finish it comfortably.*

| Week | Ride A (easy) | Ride B (endurance) | Ride C (quality) | Long ride |
|------|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------|
| 9 | 3 mi easy | 9 mi easy | 5 mi, incl. 3 × 4-min steady | 9 mi |
| 10 | 4 mi easy | **10 mi easy (first 10-miler!)** | 5 mi, incl. 4 × 4-min steady | 10 mi |

---

## Phase 4 — Build the pace (Weeks 11–14)
*Goal: now that 10 miles is in the bank, lift your sustainable speed to 15 mph.*

| Week | Ride A (easy) | Ride B (endurance) | Ride C (intervals) | Long ride |
|------|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------|
| 11 | 4 mi easy | 8 mi, middle 4 mi at steady | 5 mi, 5 × 3-min brisk (15+ mph) | 8 mi |
| 12 | 4 mi easy | 10 mi holding ~14 mph avg | 5 mi, 4 × 4-min brisk | 10 mi |
| 13 | 4 mi easy | 10 mi, push avg toward 15 mph | 5 mi, short sharp intervals | 10 mi |
| 14 (goal) | 4 mi very easy | rest or 3 mi spin | **GOAL RIDE: 10 mi @ 15 mph (40 min)** | 10 mi |

---

## A few practical notes

- **This is a guide, not a contract.** If a week feels hard, repeat it before moving on. There's no penalty for taking 16 weeks instead of 14.
- **Cadence beats force, always.** When you want to go faster, try spinning quicker before shifting to a harder gear.
- **Hydrate and fuel** on rides over ~45 minutes; bring water once your long rides pass 6–7 miles.
- **Padded shorts** make a real difference once rides get longer — saddle soreness is the other big reason beginners quit.
- **If knee pain appears:** front of knee → saddle may be slightly low; back of knee → saddle may be slightly high. Check back with your fitter.
